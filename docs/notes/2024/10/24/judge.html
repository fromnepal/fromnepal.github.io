Controversial Search Warrant and Raid on Marion County Record

Judicial and Police Actions Spark Concerns Over Press Freedom

1. **August 2023**: Magistrate Judge Laura Viar authorizes a search warrant for the Marion County Record newspaper office, based on allegations of identity theft and unlawful use of a computer.

2. **August 11, 2023**: The Marion Police Department, led by Police Chief Gideon Cody, executes the search warrant. They raid the newspaper office, the home of the publisher, and the home of a city councilwoman, seizing computers, cell phones, and other materials. This action draws widespread criticism and concerns about press freedom¹(https://www.kcur.org/news/2023-12-07/kansas-judge-who-authorized-newspaper-raid-cleared-of-incompetence-complaint).

3. **August 14, 2023**: First Amendment experts and various media organizations condemn the raid, calling it a violation of press freedom²(https://www.kcur.org/news/2023-08-14/a-police-raid-of-a-kansas-newsroom-raises-alarms-about-violations-of-press-freedom).

4. **August 16, 2023**: The Kansas Bureau of Investigation (KBI) announces it will review the circumstances surrounding the search warrant and the raid¹(https://www.kcur.org/news/2023-12-07/kansas-judge-who-authorized-newspaper-raid-cleared-of-incompetence-complaint).

5. **August 18, 2023**: The search warrant is withdrawn, and the seized items are returned to the Marion County Record. The KBI continues its investigation into the matter¹(https://www.kcur.org/news/2023-12-07/kansas-judge-who-authorized-newspaper-raid-cleared-of-incompetence-complaint).

6. **September 2023**: The Kansas Commission on Judicial Conduct begins an investigation into Judge Viar's actions in authorizing the search warrant¹(https://www.kcur.org/news/2023-12-07/kansas-judge-who-authorized-newspaper-raid-cleared-of-incompetence-complaint).

7. **October 2023**: The Kansas Commission on Judicial Conduct dismisses the complaint against Judge Viar. They conclude that while the issuance of the warrant was not "reasonable or legally appropriate," it did not constitute incompetence. The commission issues informal advice to Judge Viar to ensure more thorough reviews of laws before approving future warrants¹(https://www.kcur.org/news/2023-12-07/kansas-judge-who-authorized-newspaper-raid-cleared-of-incompetence-complaint)³(https://www.yahoo.com/news/updates-kansas-newspaper-raid-search-192927283.html).

8. **December 2023**: A new lawsuit is filed against Marion Police Chief Gideon Cody, alleging that the raid was conducted in retaliation against the journalists¹(https://www.kcur.org/news/2023-12-07/kansas-judge-who-authorized-newspaper-raid-cleared-of-incompetence-complaint).

9. **April 2024**: A Topeka woman files a complaint against Judge Viar with the Kansas Commission on Judicial Conduct after reading about the lawsuit against Chief Cody³(https://www.yahoo.com/news/updates-kansas-newspaper-raid-search-192927283.html).

10. **August 2024**: The Kansas Commission on Judicial Conduct dismisses the complaint against Judge Viar, citing insufficient evidence to conclude incompetence. The panel members include District Judge Bradley Ambrosier, Kansas Court of Appeals Judge Thomas Malone, and others³(https://www.yahoo.com/news/updates-kansas-newspaper-raid-search-192927283.html).

11. **Ongoing**: The incident continues to attract national and international attention, raising significant concerns about judicial oversight, police conduct, and the protection of First Amendment rights¹(https://www.kcur.org/news/2023-12-07/kansas-judge-who-authorized-newspaper-raid-cleared-of-incompetence-complaint)²(https://www.kcur.org/news/2023-08-14/a-police-raid-of-a-kansas-newsroom-raises-alarms-about-violations-of-press-freedom).
