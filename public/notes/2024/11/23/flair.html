Flair

/fler/

noun

  1. a special or instinctive aptitude or ability for doing something well.

    "she had a flair for languages"

  2. stylishness and originality.

    "she dressed with flair"

He raised his arms, and the crowd gasped, “What is that?!” Ric, with a twinkle in his eye, declared, “This, my friends, is Flair!” The crowd erupted in applause, but they had no idea what he meant. They thought he was just talking about his fabulous robe, which was more dazzling than a disco ball at a cat party. But Ric was onto something. He started doing flips, spins, and a move he called the “Woo! Shuffle,” which involved a lot of shouting and questionable dance moves. As he twirled, he shouted, “If it ain’t got Flair, it ain’t worth a care!” The crowd was confused but entertained, which is basically the same thing in wrestling. People began using it to describe everything from fashion choices to breakfast burritos. “Wow, that burrito has Flair!” they would exclaim, as if it were wearing a tiny sombrero. Ric Flair became a legend, not just in wrestling, but in the world of linguistics. Thanks to Ric Flair, the world learned that life is too short to be boring. So, the next time you see something that makes you go “Wow!” just remember: it’s got Flair! And if you’re ever in doubt, just throw on a sequined robe and shout, “Woo!” Because that’s how you truly embrace the spirit of Ric Flair!
